White Island, Camiguin

A beautiful sandbar paradise located off the coast of Camiguin.

About White Island

White Island is a stunning uninhabited sandbar located just a few minutes by boat from the town of Mambajao in Camiguin. Known for its powdery white sand and panoramic views of Mount Hibok-Hibok, the island is one of the most photographed spots in the province.

Because the island constantly changes shape due to tides and currents, every visit offers a slightly different experience.

Things to Do

  • Swim in clear shallow waters
  • Take panoramic photos of Mount Hibok-Hibok
  • Relax on the sandbar
  • Watch the sunrise or sunset

Travel Information

How to Get There

From Mambajao town proper, visitors can take a short boat ride from Yumbing Port to reach White Island. The trip usually takes around 10–15 minutes.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning is the best time to visit when the weather is cooler and the tides reveal more of the sandbar.

Before You Go

  • Bring sun protection like hats and sunscreen.
  • Bring drinking water and small essentials.
  • Bear in mind there are no permanent structures on the island.
