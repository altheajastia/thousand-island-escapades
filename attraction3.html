Sunken Cemetery, Camiguin

A historic and peaceful landmark where memory, sea, and scenery meet.

About Sunken Cemetery

Sunken Cemetery is one of the most unique attractions in Camiguin. It was once a burial ground, but after a volcanic eruption in the 1870s, it sank beneath the sea. Today, a large cross stands at the site as a memorial landmark, marking the area and creating one of the island's most recognizable views.

More than just a tourist destination, Sunken Cemetery tells a story of Camiguin's history and resilience. Visitors are drawn not only by its scenic beauty, but also by its peaceful atmosphere and the cultural significance of the landmark.

What You Can Do

  • Enjoy the sunset and coastal view
  • Ride a small boat around the site
  • Take photos of the famous cross by the sea
  • Snorkel to explore the coral-covered graves
  • Learn more about Camiguin's volcanic history

Travel Information

Location

Sunken Cemetery is located in the town of Catarman, Camiguin. It is easy to reach by tricycle, motorcycle, or multicab from nearby towns.

Best Time to Visit

Late afternoon is one of the best times to visit because the sunset adds a dramatic and beautiful glow to the area. Morning visits are also good for a quieter experience.

Reminders for Visitors

  • Bring a camera or phone for pictures.
  • Wear comfortable clothes for warm weather.
  • Be careful near the water and boat area.
  • Respect the place because it is a historical site.
